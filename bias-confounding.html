STEP 8 / 9

偏差、干擾與批次效應

即使統計再嚴謹，未控制的偏差也能讓結論完全顛倒——這就是流行病學最常見的論文撤稿原因。

No statistic survives uncontrolled bias. It is the most common reason for epidemiologic retractions.

概覽

偏差的三大來源

傳統流行病學把偏差分三類：

  • Selection bias（選擇偏倚）：誰被招募進來、誰失訪不對等
  • Information bias（資訊偏倚）：暴露或結果測量不準確或不對稱
  • Confounding（干擾）：第三變數同時與暴露和結果相關

生醫資訊時代又增加一項：Batch effect（批次效應）——技術變數（如定序中心、年份）混淆了真正的生物訊號。

Classical epidemiology recognizes three biases:

  • Selection bias — who gets in, who drops out
  • Information bias — measurement is inaccurate or asymmetric
  • Confounding — a third variable is associated with both exposure and outcome

Biomedical informatics adds: Batch effects — technical covariates (sequencing center, year) confound true biological signal.

Selection Bias

一、選擇偏倚常見型態

🏥 Berkson 偏倚

醫院 case-control 研究中，住院病人比一般人更常合併多種疾病，導致兩疾病出現虛假關聯。對策：盡量從族群而非醫院抽樣。

Hospital-based case-control studies inflate comorbidity associations because hospitalized patients have more conditions. Fix: sample from population, not hospitals.

🚪 健康工人效應

職業 cohort 中，員工通常比一般族群健康（因疾病者已離職）→ 低估暴露的不良影響。對策：與內部對照比較或標準化死亡比 SMR。

Workers are healthier than the general population (sick leave the workforce), underestimating exposure harms. Fix: compare to internal controls or use SMR.

📞 不回應偏倚

填問卷的人通常比不填的人更健康/教育程度高 → 估計值有偏。對策：multiple imputation、敏感度分析、提高回應率。

Respondents are typically healthier / more educated than non-respondents → biased estimates. Fix: multiple imputation, sensitivity analysis, boost response rates.

🎯 碰撞變數偏倚

調整一個被兩變數共同影響的「碰撞節點」會造成假關聯。例如 COVID 研究中「住院」即為 collider，分層分析住院族群會引入新偏倚。

Conditioning on a variable affected by two others creates spurious associations. In COVID studies, "hospitalization" is a collider; stratifying by it introduces new bias.

Confounding

二、干擾與辨識

一個變數要成為干擾因子，必須同時滿足三條件：

  1. 與暴露相關（A → C）
  2. 與結果相關（C → Y），且不在「暴露 → 結果」的中介路徑上
  3. 不是暴露的後果

經典例子：「咖啡飲用」與「肺癌」的關聯——年代久遠的研究發現喝咖啡的人肺癌較多。但喝咖啡者也常抽菸，抽菸才是真正的因子（同時與咖啡與肺癌相關）。

To be a confounder, a variable must satisfy three conditions:

  1. Associated with the exposure (A → C)
  2. Associated with the outcome (C → Y) — and not on the causal pathway
  3. Not a consequence of the exposure

Classic example: "Coffee" appeared linked to "lung cancer" in older studies. But coffee drinkers smoked more, and smoking is the real driver (associated with both coffee and lung cancer).

A (Coffee) ───────────► Y (Lung Cancer)
    ▲                             ▲
    └────── C (Smoking) ──────┘

三種處理策略：

  • 設計階段：隨機化 (RCT)、限制 (restriction)、配對 (matching)
  • 分析階段：分層 (stratification)、多變量回歸、propensity score
  • 進階：工具變數、Mendelian randomization（詳見 Step 10）

Three mitigation strategies:

  • Design stage: randomization (RCT), restriction, matching
  • Analysis stage: stratification, multivariable regression, propensity score
  • Advanced: instrumental variables, Mendelian randomization (see Step 10)
Batch Effects

三、組學的批次效應

2010 年 Leek et al. 在 Nature Reviews Genetics 揭露：許多公開 microarray 研究的「生物學發現」其實主要由定序日期驅動。批次效應的來源包括：

  • 不同實驗室、技術員、機器
  • 不同 reagent lot、protocol 版本
  • 不同年份、季節（試劑批次）
  • 不同樣本處理日期或冷凍時間

診斷工具：PCA + 用 batch 變數上色看是否分層；plotMD、relative log expression (RLE) plots。

Leek et al. (Nat Rev Genet 2010) showed that many "biological findings" in public microarray data were driven by processing date. Sources of batch effects:

  • Different labs, technicians, machines
  • Different reagent lots, protocol versions
  • Different years/seasons
  • Different sample handling or freezing dates

Diagnostics: PCA colored by batch (look for stratification), plotMD, RLE plots.

⚠️
實驗設計才是根本：校正再強也救不回「所有 case 在 batch 1，所有 control 在 batch 2」的完全混淆設計。樣本應在 batch 間平衡分配。 Design beats correction: No method can rescue a fully confounded design ("all cases in batch 1, all controls in batch 2"). Balance samples across batches at the design stage.
程式碼

實作範例

# Detect batch via PCA
pca <- prcomp(t(expr), scale.=TRUE)
plot(pca$x, col=factor(meta$batch))   # color by batch

# ComBat from sva
library(sva)
modcombat <- model.matrix(~ disease, data=meta)  # keep biology
expr_adj  <- ComBat(dat=expr,
                    batch=meta$batch,
                    mod=modcombat)
library(limma)

# Remove batch for downstream visualization (PCA, heatmap)
expr_adj <- removeBatchEffect(expr,
              batch=meta$batch,
              design=model.matrix(~ meta$disease))

# For statistical testing: include batch as covariate instead!
design <- model.matrix(~ disease + batch, data=meta)
fit <- lmFit(expr, design); fit <- eBayes(fit)
topTable(fit, coef="disease")
import pandas as pd
from combat.pycombat import pycombat   # pip install combat

# expr: genes x samples; batch / biology as Series
expr_adj = pycombat(expr, batch=meta["batch"],
                    mod=pd.get_dummies(meta["disease"]))

# For single-cell, use scanpy + Harmony / Scanorama / scVI
import scanpy as sc, harmonypy as hm
sc.tl.pca(adata)
ho = hm.run_harmony(adata.obsm["X_pca"], adata.obs, ["batch"])
adata.obsm["X_pca_harmony"] = ho.Z_corr.T

📝 隨堂測驗

Q1. 干擾因子的三個條件？

A. 與暴露相關、與結果相關、不在因果鏈中介Associated with exposure, with outcome, not a mediator
B. 與暴露無關、與結果相關、是 colliderUnrelated to exposure, related to outcome, is a collider
C. 是暴露的後果、與結果無關、是中介Consequence of exposure, unrelated to outcome, is a mediator
D. 是隨機變數、有大樣本量、是 confounderRandom, large n, is a confounder
三條件缺一不可：與 exposure 相關、與 outcome 相關、不是中介或暴露的後果。All three are required: associated with exposure, with outcome, and not a mediator or consequence.

Q2. 已知 batch 最常用？

A. SVA
B. ComBat
C. PCA
D. 不校正No correction
ComBat (Johnson 2007) 是處理已知 batch 變數的金標準 empirical-Bayes 方法。ComBat (Johnson 2007) is the gold-standard empirical-Bayes method when batch is known.

Q3. 完全混淆設計可以靠校正補救嗎？

A. 可以，用 ComBatYes, with ComBat
B. 不可以——biology 與 batch 完全混淆No — biology and batch are perfectly confounded
C. 可以，用更多樣本Yes, with more samples
D. 可以，用 SVAYes, with SVA
當生物變數與 batch 100% 相關時，沒有任何方法能分離兩者效應。應在設計階段 balance。When biology is 100% correlated with batch, no method can separate them. Balance at the design stage.

Q4. Berkson 偏倚屬於？

A. Selection bias
B. Information bias
C. Confounding
D. Batch effect
Berkson 偏倚出現在醫院樣本選擇上，住院病人比一般人更常合併多種疾病，是典型 selection bias。Berkson's bias arises from hospital sampling — hospitalized patients have more comorbidities. It is selection bias.
